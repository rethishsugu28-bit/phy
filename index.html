Comprehensive Development Report

Electromagnetic Induction-Based Wireless Charging System

This report details the design, construction, and rigorous testing of a low-power Wire-less Power Transfer (WPT) system centered on the principle of **Electromagnetic Induction (EMI)**. The system successfully demonstrated contactless energy transmission between a primary (transmitter) resonant coil and a secondary (receiver) coil.

Project Team: Rethish, Praveen, Vishal, Samiksha, Yuvasree, Selvapriya, Pavatharani

Date: November 2025

The Objective: Near-Field Inductive Coupling

The primary objective was to design and build a functional, small-scale prototype for wireless power transfer (WPT) using near-field inductive coupling. The system was designed to reliably illuminate an LED or charge a small electronic load over a short distance (~5-10 mm) while providing empirical validation of the underlying physics.

Mechanism & Design Choices

Fundamental Principle

The system relies on **Faraday's Law of Induction**. A higher rate of change of magnetic flux (i.e., higher operating frequency) results in a greater induced voltage.

Transmitter Circuit

A **555 Timer IC** was configured in astable mode to generate a precise square wave oscillation in the range of **100 kHz to 200 kHz**. This high frequency minimizes the required inductance value.

Receiver Circuit

A **Full-Wave Bridge Rectifier** utilizing high-speed **Schottky Diodes** was implemented. Schottky diodes were chosen for their lower forward voltage drop (~0.3 V), minimizing power loss.

Empirical Performance and Validation

~78%

**Maximum Power Transfer Efficiency ($\eta_{max}$)** achieved at the closest separation (0 mm gap). This is a strong result for a non-optimized, low-power prototype.

Key Observations

  • **Output Voltage:** A stable **5.0 V DC output** was achieved across a constant resistive load on the secondary side.
  • **Range Constraint:** Power transfer dropped below the required threshold (4.5 V) beyond **~15 mm separation**.
  • **Alignment:** The system maintained over 70% of its maximum output voltage with up to **5 mm of lateral misalignment**.

Future Enhancements for Commercial Viability

The system's current performance validates the theoretical dependence of efficiency on high-frequency oscillation and critical coil alignment. Future work will focus on three major enhancements to improve real-world usability:

1. Resonant Matching Optimization

Implementation of a precise, coupled **LC-Tank Resonant Circuit** on both sides. This would significantly increase the Quality factor ($Q$) of the coils and extend the functional charging range up to **50-100 percent**.

2. Foreign Object Detection (FOD)

Integrating a mechanism to detect metallic objects between the coils is crucial for safety. This prevents power loss due to eddy currents and localized heating.

3. Feedback and Power Control

Developing a simple communication channel would allow the receiver to request variable power levels. This approach will maximize efficiency and reduce standby power consumption.
